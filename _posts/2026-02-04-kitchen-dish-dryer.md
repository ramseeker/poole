## Key Features to Consider:

- **Size & Capacity**: Measure your counter space before buying. Standard racks (12-14 inches) hold 8-12 dishes. Large racks (16-18 inches) accommodate families. Consider vertical 2-tier racks to maximize capacity without using more counter space.

- **Drainage System**: Look for sloped drainboards or swivel spouts that direct water into the sink, preventing counter puddles. Some premium models have self-draining systems. Mats absorb water but need frequent washing.

- **Material Quality**: Stainless steel (especially 304 grade) resists rust and lasts years. Coated steel chips over time. Plastic is lightweight but less durable. Silicone is heat-resistant and easy to clean.

- **Included Accessories**: Check for utensil holders, cup holders, wine glass holders, and knife blocks. Removable components offer flexibility. Some include cutting board racks. Premium racks have integrated features.

- **Space-Saving Options**: Over-sink racks free up counter space entirely. Foldable/collapsible racks store flat when not needed. Roll-up mats fit in drawers. Consider your kitchen layout and storage constraints.

- **Maintenance**: Removable trays simplify cleaning. Dishwasher-safe components save time. Anti-residue coatings prevent mineral buildup in hard water areas. Microfiber mats are machine washable.
