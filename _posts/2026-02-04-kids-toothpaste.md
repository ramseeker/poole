## Key Features to Consider:

- **Fluoride vs Fluoride-Free**: Fluoride toothpaste is the ADA gold standard for cavity prevention and is recommended for most children ages 2+. Fluoride-free options using hydroxyapatite or xylitol work well for children who can't spit yet or families preferring natural alternatives. Consult your pediatric dentist.

- **Age Appropriateness**: Use training toothpaste (fluoride-free, safe to swallow) for babies and toddlers under 2 who haven't learned to spit. Switch to fluoride toothpaste around age 2, using only a pea-sized amount until age 6 when kids can reliably spit.

- **Flavor Selection**: Fun flavors like strawberry, bubblegum, watermelon, and fruit make brushing enjoyable. Avoid mint for very young children as it can be too strong. Let kids help choose their flavor to increase compliance.

- **ADA Seal of Acceptance**: Look for this seal on fluoride toothpastes to ensure the product meets safety and efficacy standards. The ADA evaluates products based on scientific evidence for cavity prevention.

- **Special Ingredients**: Hydroxyapatite is a natural mineral that remineralizes teeth without fluoride. Xylitol is a natural sweetener that reduces cavity-causing bacteria. Both are excellent alternatives for fluoride-free options.

- **Sensory-Friendly Options**: Some children have sensory sensitivities to taste, texture, or foaming. Look for unflavored options, low-foam formulas, or mild flavors. SLS-free toothpastes foam less and may be better tolerated.
