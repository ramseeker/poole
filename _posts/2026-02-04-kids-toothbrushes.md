## Key Features to Consider:

- **Age Appropriateness**: Match the toothbrush to your child's developmental stage. Babies need silicone finger brushes or ultra-soft infant brushes, toddlers need small heads with soft bristles, and older kids can handle standard kids' brushes with slightly firmer bristles.

- **Electric vs Manual**: Electric toothbrushes can make brushing more fun and may provide better plaque removal, but they're more expensive. Manual toothbrushes work well when used properly and are great for teaching technique. Consider starting with manual and upgrading to electric around age 3-5.

- **Bristle Softness**: Always choose soft or extra-soft bristles for children. Hard bristles can damage developing enamel and sensitive gum tissue. Replace toothbrushes every 3 months or when bristles become frayed.

- **Fun Characters & Colors**: Licensed characters (Disney, Minecraft, Barbie, Bluey) make brushing more appealing to reluctant brushers. Bright colors and fun designs encourage kids to look forward to brushing time.

- **Special Features**: Timers (built-in or LED) help kids brush for the recommended 2 minutes. Suction cups keep brushes upright and hygienic. Travel covers protect bristles on the go. 360-degree bristles make it easier for young children to brush effectively.

- **Grip & Handle Design**: Look for easy-grip handles sized for small hands. Chunky handles help toddlers developing fine motor skills. Ergonomic designs prevent hand fatigue during brushing.
