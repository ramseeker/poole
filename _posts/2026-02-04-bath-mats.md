## Key Features to Consider:

- **Material Type**: Chenille offers plush comfort and absorbency, memory foam provides cushioning, cotton is natural and traditional, while stone/diatomite excels at rapid drying. Choose based on your comfort preferences and bathroom humidity levels.

- **Size and Coverage**: Measure your space carefully. Standard 24x17 inch mats work for most showers, while 30x20 to 36x24 inch options provide more coverage. Runners (50-60 inches) are ideal for double vanities or long bathroom layouts.

- **Non-Slip Backing**: Essential for safety. Look for rubber, silicone dots, or suction cups depending on placement. TP rubber backing is more durable than PVC or hot glue, especially for frequently washed mats.

- **Quick-Dry vs. Plush**: High-pile chenille and memory foam feel luxurious but take longer to dry. Thin mats, waffle textures, and stone materials dry faster, making them better for humid bathrooms without ventilation.

- **Machine Washability**: Most fabric mats are machine washable, but check care instructions. Stone mats simply wipe clean. For high-traffic bathrooms, choose fade-resistant materials that withstand frequent washing.

- **Special Features**: Consider sets for complete bathroom coordination, U-shaped mats for toilets, tub mats with suction cups for safety, or foldable options for easy storage and travel.
