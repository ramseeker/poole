layout: list
title: "Saturday Afternoon Gardening: May Beginner (Small Yard)"
intro: "A no-stress shopping list for a first-time May gardener with limited space. Focuses on plants and tools that work in pots or small beds—no lawn conversion required."
items:
  - name: "2–3 nursery starts of patio tomato or bush zucchini"
    desc: "Look for tags that say 'patio,' 'bush,' or 'determinate'—these stay under 2 feet tall and grow fine in a 12-inch pot."
  - name: "One 8–10 quart bag of potting mix (container formula)"
    desc: "Avoid 'garden soil' or 'topsoil'—those turn into brick in pots; container mix has perlite for drainage."
  - name: "Two 14–16 inch pots with drainage holes"
    desc: "Plastic is lighter and cheaper, terra cotta breathes better—either works as long as water can exit the bottom."
  - name: "One 2-gallon watering can with a removable rose"
    desc: "The rose (sprinkler head) turns a harsh stream into a soft rain that won't flatten seedlings or blast soil out."
  - name: "One hand trowel with a metal blade"
    desc: "You'll use this for every hole, every weed, and every scoop of soil—skip the wide plastic ones that bend."
  - name: "One bag of slow-release granular fertilizer (5-5-5 or similar)"
    desc: "Mix one tablespoon into each pot at planting time, then ignore feeding for the next 6 weeks."
  - name: "One 6-pack of marigold seedlings"
    desc: "Tuck one marigold into each vegetable pot—their roots help repel soil pests and the flowers bring bees."
  - name: "Two 3-foot bamboo stakes and soft twist ties"
    desc: "Push a stake into each tomato or pepper pot now, before the plant flops; ties go on loosely as the stem grows."
optional:
  - name: "Small kneeling pad or a folded yoga mat"
    desc: "Worth the $5 if your small yard is mostly patio bricks or compacted dirt."
  - name: "One mesh produce bag (like onion bag)"
    desc: "Cut it into a flat square and lay over zucchini seedlings—stops squash vine borers while letting sun through."
tips:
  - "Water at the base, not over the leaves. Wet leaves overnight invite mildew; wet leaves at noon act like magnifying glasses."
  - "Don't buy a hose timer or drip system yet. For 2–4 pots, a watering can and 3 minutes a day is plenty."
  - "Pick off yellow lower leaves once a week. That one simple habit prevents most fungal problems in a small space."
  - "If a plant looks wilty after transplanting, move the pot to morning sun only (east side of house) for 3 days—don't overwater it."
```
