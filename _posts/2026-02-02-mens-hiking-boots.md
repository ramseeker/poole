## Key Features to Consider:

- **Waterproofing**: Gore-Tex remains the gold standard, but proprietary membranes from brands like Keen and Columbia offer excellent protection at lower price points.
- **Ankle Support**: Mid-cut boots provide ankle stability without the bulk of high-cut models, ideal for day hikes and moderate terrain.
- **Traction**: Vibram outsoles deliver superior grip on wet and dry surfaces, while brand-specific rubber compounds offer good performance at reduced costs.
- **Break-In Period**: Modern synthetic boots require minimal break-in, while leather models may need 10-20 miles to fully conform to your feet.
- **Weight**: Lightweight boots (2-3 lbs per pair) reduce fatigue on long hikes, though heavier boots often provide better durability and support.
- **Fit**: Wide toe boxes prevent blisters and accommodate thicker socks, while secure heel cups prevent slippage on steep descents.
- **Breathability**: Look for mesh panels and breathable membranes if hiking in warm climates or during summer months.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
