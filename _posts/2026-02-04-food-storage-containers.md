## Key Features to Consider:

- **Material Safety**: Glass and stainless steel are the safest nontoxic options that don't leach chemicals. If choosing plastic, look for BPA-free and ideally PFAS-free containers. Avoid heating plastic when possible to minimize chemical transfer.

- **Leak-Proof vs. Airtight**: Leak-proof containers prevent liquid spills during transport. Airtight containers seal out air to keep food fresh longer. The best containers offer both features with snap-lock or silicone-sealed lids.

- **Temperature Range**: Glass and stainless steel handle extreme temperatures from freezer to oven. Plastic containers vary—most are microwave and freezer safe but check oven compatibility. Borosilicate glass resists thermal shock better than standard glass.

- **Size & Portability**: Individual meal prep containers (16-32 oz) work for lunches. Family-size containers (4-8 cups) suit leftovers and batch cooking. Pantry canisters need larger capacities (1-4 liters) for bulk dry goods.

- **Stackability & Storage**: Containers that nest or stack save cabinet and fridge space. Check that lids store conveniently—some designs have lids that attach to container bottoms or snap together separately.

- **Specialized Features**: Fruit/vegetable containers with colanders maintain freshness. Vacuum seal systems extend shelf life. Compartmented containers separate foods. Measurement markings help with portion control.
