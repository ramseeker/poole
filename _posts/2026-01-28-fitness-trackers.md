## Key Features to Consider:

- **Tracking Capabilities**: Basic models count steps and calories, mid-range add heart rate and sleep tracking, premium include GPS, SpO2, stress, and recovery metrics
- **Battery Life Differences**: Budget trackers last 5-10 days, premium Fitbit/Garmin offer 10-30 days, while Garmin Vivofit provides 1+ year with replaceable battery
- **Water Resistance Levels**: 3 ATM handles rain and handwashing, 5 ATM suitable for swimming, 10 ATM allows snorkeling and water sports activities
- **Display Options**: AMOLED screens offer vibrant colors and readability, always-on displays show time constantly, while e-ink extends battery life significantly
- **Smart Features Integration**: Notifications for calls and messages, music control, contactless payments, and voice assistants enhance daily convenience beyond fitness
- **Health Monitoring Depth**: Advanced trackers measure HRV, body battery, readiness scores, menstrual cycle, and stress to provide holistic wellness insights
- **Compatibility Requirements**: Most work with both iOS and Android, but some features (Apple Health sync, Google Fit) may be platform-specific or require subscriptions

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
