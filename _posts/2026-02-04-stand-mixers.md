## Key Features to Consider:

- **Bowl Capacity**: 3-4.5 qt suits 1-2 people, 5-6 qt works for families, 7+ qt for large batches or entertaining. Consider your typical recipe sizes. Smaller bowls struggle with tiny batches; larger ones don't mix small amounts well.

- **Motor Power**: 250-300 watts handles light mixing and small batches. 500-600 watts tackles bread dough and heavy batters. 1000+ watts is commercial-grade for constant use. More power = better performance with thick dough.

- **Tilt-Head vs Bowl-Lift**: Tilt-head (3-6 qt) tilts back for easy access, simpler and usually cheaper. Bowl-lift (5-8 qt) raises bowl to blades, more stable for heavy dough, professional style. Choose based on capacity needs.

- **Speed Settings**: 6-10 speeds handle most tasks. Lower speeds prevent splatter with dry ingredients. Higher speeds for whipping cream. Soft start feature gradually increases speed to prevent flour clouds.

- **Attachments Included**: Standard includes flat beater (general mixing), wire whip (aeration), and dough hook (kneading). Some include flex edge beaters that scrape bowl sides. Check what comes with base model vs sold separately.

- **Attachment Hub**: KitchenAid's universal hub accepts 15+ optional attachments (pasta maker, meat grinder, ice cream maker, spiralizer). Other brands have limited or no attachment ecosystems. Consider future versatility.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
