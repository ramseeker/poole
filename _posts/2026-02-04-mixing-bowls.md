## Key Features to Consider:

- **Material Choice**: Stainless steel is durable, lightweight, and won't break. Glass allows you to see contents and is microwave/oven safe. Plastic is affordable and unbreakable. Ceramic/porcelain is attractive for serving but heavier and breakable.

- **Nesting Ability**: Nesting bowls save significant cabinet space. Check that bowls truly nest compactly inside each other. Some designs look like they nest but actually require more space than expected.

- **Lids & Storage**: Airtight lids transform mixing bowls into food storage containers. Verify lids are truly airtight for refrigerator storage. Some lids are microwave-safe while others must be removed before heating.

- **Non-Slip Features**: Silicone or rubber bottoms prevent bowls from sliding during vigorous mixing, whisking, or beating. This is especially important for stand mixer users and when making bread dough.

- **Size Range**: A good set includes small (1-2 qt) for dressings and small batches, medium (3-4 qt) for general mixing, and large (5+ qt) for big recipes. Consider your typical cooking volume when selecting sizes.

- **Special Features**: Some sets include grater attachments, measurement markings, pour spouts, colanders, or matching utensils. These extras can replace multiple kitchen tools and increase value.
