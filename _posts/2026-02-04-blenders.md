## Key Features to Consider:

- **Motor Power**: Personal blenders (200-500 watts) work for soft fruits. Mid-range (600-1000 watts) handle ice and frozen fruit. High-performance (1000+ watts) blend tough ingredients like nuts, seeds, and make hot soup through friction.

- **Container Material**: Glass jars don't scratch or stain but are heavy and breakable. BPA-free plastic (Tritan) is lightweight and durable. Stainless steel retains temperature but you can't see contents. Size matters—personal (12-24 oz), standard (40-64 oz), or commercial (72+ oz).

- **Blade Design**: Fixed blades are easier to clean but less versatile. Removable blades offer more options but create cleaning hassle. Serrated blades grip ingredients better. Multiple blade levels blend more thoroughly.

- **Speed Control**: Variable speed offers precision for different textures. Preset programs (smoothie, ice crush, soup) simplify operation. Pulse function provides burst blending control. High-end models offer 10+ speeds.

- **Special Features**: Self-cleaning saves time—add water and soap, blend. Tamper helps process thick mixtures. Heating elements make hot soup. Smart connectivity tracks nutrition. Noise level matters for apartments.

- **Warranty & Durability**: Budget blenders offer 1-3 year warranties. Mid-range provide 5-7 years. Premium brands like Vitamix offer 7-10 years, indicating superior build quality and customer support.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
