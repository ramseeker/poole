## Key Features to Consider:

- **Stainless Steel Grade**: 18/10 stainless steel (18% chromium, 10% nickel) offers superior rust resistance and durability compared to 18/0. The nickel adds shine and corrosion protection. Premium sets use 18/10, while budget options often use 18/0.

- **Weight & Balance**: Quality flatware should feel substantial in hand without being too heavy. Balanced weight distribution between handle and head prevents tipping. Heavier sets (around 2.5-3 oz per piece) often indicate better quality.

- **Finish Options**: Mirror polish provides classic shine and elegance. Satin/matte finishes hide fingerprints and scratches better. Gold finishes (PVD coating) add luxury but may require hand washing to maintain appearance.

- **Service Size**: 20-piece sets serve 4 people with 5 pieces each (dinner fork, salad fork, knife, tablespoon, teaspoon). 40-piece sets serve 8. Consider your household size and entertaining frequency when selecting.

- **Steak Knives**: Sets with steak knives (24, 48, or more pieces) provide serrated blades for cutting meat. Check if knives are forged or stamped—forged knives offer better quality and durability.

- **Serving Utensils**: Complete dining sets include serving spoons, slotted spoons, forks, and specialty tools. If your set doesn't include these, purchase separately or choose a larger piece count that includes them.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
