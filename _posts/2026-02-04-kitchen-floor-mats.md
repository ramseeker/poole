## Key Features to Consider:

- **Thickness & Cushioning**: Mats range from 1/2 inch to 9/10 inch thick. Thicker mats (3/4 inch or more) provide better anti-fatigue relief for extended standing, while thinner mats work better under low-clearance appliances or doors.

- **Material Type**: Memory foam offers superior comfort and conforms to your feet, while PVC and rubber provide more durability and easier cleaning. Premium gel-filled mats like GelPro offer the best of both worlds but at higher prices.

- **Waterproof vs. Washable**: Waterproof mats can be wiped clean and resist spills, ideal for messy cooking areas. Washable fabric mats are softer underfoot but require machine washing and may not handle major spills as well.

- **Size & Coverage**: Measure your standing area carefully. Standard mats (17x28 to 20x32 inches) work for single standing spots. Runners (47-60 inches long) are better for galley kitchens or areas in front of long counters. Two-piece sets provide flexibility for multiple work zones.

- **Non-Slip Backing**: Essential for safety on smooth kitchen floors. Look for textured rubber or PVC backing with ridges or suction patterns. Test on your specific floor type, as some backings work better on tile vs. hardwood.

- **Style & Décor**: Anti-fatigue mats now come in decorative farmhouse, boho, and vintage styles that coordinate with kitchen décor, not just basic black or grey. Consider whether you want purely functional or a mat that enhances your kitchen aesthetic.
