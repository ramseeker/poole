## Key Features to Consider:

- **Electric vs. Stovetop**: Electric kettles boil water faster (3-5 minutes) with auto shut-off safety. Stovetop kettles work during power outages, last longer, and often cost less. Choose electric for speed and convenience, stovetop for durability.

- **Capacity**: Small kettles (0.5-1L) suit 1-2 people or travel. Standard size (1.5-1.8L) serves 4-6 cups. Large capacity (2.5L+) works for families or entertaining. Consider your typical usage and counter/storage space.

- **Temperature Control**: Variable temperature kettles ($50-150) let you set precise temps for different teas (160-212°F). Essential for tea enthusiasts. Basic kettles just boil to 212°F, which works fine for coffee and most uses.

- **Material**: Stainless steel is durable and retains heat well. Glass lets you see water level and looks elegant but can break. Ceramic/enamel offers vintage aesthetics. BPA-free plastic is lightweight for travel.

- **Gooseneck Spout**: Pour-over coffee enthusiasts need gooseneck spouts for precise, controlled pouring. Standard spouts work fine for general tea and coffee making. Gooseneck kettles cost $50-180.

- **Safety Features**: Auto shut-off prevents dry boiling damage. Boil-dry protection turns off kettle if water runs low. Cool-touch handles and bases prevent burns. Essential for households with children.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
