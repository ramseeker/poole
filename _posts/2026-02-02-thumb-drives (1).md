## Key Features to Consider:

- **Speed Class**: USB 3.0 offers 5Gbps, USB 3.1/3.2 Gen 1 matches that, while Gen 2 doubles to 10Gbps. Real-world speeds typically reach 60-70% of theoretical maximums.
- **Capacity Needs**: 64GB suits casual users, 128GB hits the sweet spot for most, 256GB+ serves content creators and power users requiring ample storage.
- **Connector Type**: USB-A remains universal, USB-C is future-proof, dual-connector drives offer maximum versatility across devices.
- **Build Quality**: Metal casings provide durability and heat dissipation, while capless or swivel designs eliminate lost caps.
- **Warranty Coverage**: Premium brands like Samsung and SanDisk offer 5-year to lifetime warranties, budget options typically provide 1-2 years.
- **Security Features**: Password protection and hardware encryption essential for sensitive data, though they may reduce transfer speeds slightly.
- **Form Factor**: Ultra-compact drives stay plugged in devices, standard sizes offer easier handling and better cooling.

All prices are approximate and may vary based on sales and availability.
